Run of Show: OpenAI vs. Elon, AI FDA, Coinbase Layoffs, Digesting Meta Earnings 
by John Coogan
OpenAI vs. Elon Trial Continues
It’s week two now and the Oakland courthouse continues to deliver top-tier tech drama. Elon testified for more than seven hours last week, now Greg Brockman is getting grilled over personal financial incentives, his $30B OpenAI stake, and links to various angel investments.
Mike Isaac has a great play-by-play [ https://substack.com/redirect/3e6f5e27-5245-420c-890f-1ebc7de75238?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] of the Brockman testimony.
U.S. Government Will Review AI Models
OpenAI and Anthropic actually signed on two years ago to give the U.S. government early access to unreleased frontier AI models for national-security testing. Now, Google, Microsoft, and xAI have agreed as well [ https://substack.com/redirect/7649e20a-88a7-4d51-871d-767d4b8f715e?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ]. I’m wondering why Meta is not in on this? The Commerce Department’s AI safety body can test versions with reduced or disabled safeguards to test for dangerous capabilities like cyber, bio, or national security risks. Probably good news for everyone? (George Hotz doesn’t think so. [ https://substack.com/redirect/02f40a0c-aa93-4070-9a75-bb5cd4d81957?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ])
Coinbase Lays Off 14% of Staff
Coinbase is cutting about 700 employees, or roughly 14% of its workforce [ https://substack.com/redirect/f462f766-7497-4c8d-926d-98441cccb4a3?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ], as Brian Armstrong tries to flatten the company, reduce costs, and push harder into AI-driven productivity. Questions about the framing: AI is not the number one cause, and the team is not insanely huge, but the goal is clearly lightly AI-enabled leanness. Fewer layers and managers, larger teams and more individual-contributions. Some debate here over crypto market cycles and the realities of managing a large organization. Is it actually underrated to be able to scale a workforce up and down quickly?
Digesting Meta Earnings
Meta’s core ad business is ripping, Q1 up 33%. Strong ad impressions and margins, but the market is worried that the beautiful cash machine is turning into an AI capex furnace. $125-145B is a lot of capex, especially for a company without a cloud business that can resell capacity. Even xAI is finding it hard to drive demand for near-frontier, but slightly lagging models, but Elon has moved quickly to solve the problem with a Cursor partnership. The stock is cheap right now, but investors are unclear about the payoff around all the AI spend. [ https://substack.com/redirect/f01d32c0-42b1-4b7d-9c32-be82366f5991?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] There’s also legal/regulatory risk. The cash machine is a really good cash machine though.
