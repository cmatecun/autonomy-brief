In today’s episode, I’m speaking with Crijn Bouman [ https://substack.com/redirect/45783c82-85ac-4ca8-96d2-fedbf4355c3d?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ], CEO and Co-founder of Rocsys [ https://substack.com/redirect/71f7c7ae-7a23-4d8f-9193-fc5fc4b888f0?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ]. Crijn shares his background in the EV charging industry, what led him to found Rocsys, and why he believes charging has become one of the biggest operational bottlenecks for autonomous vehicle fleets. We discuss why depot operations deserve far more attention as robotaxi fleets scale, how much of fleet management can ultimately be automated, and why charging is the logical place to start.
Crijn also discusses the Rocsys M1, the company’s hands-free charging solution designed specifically for autonomous fleets. We explore how the system works, why it is designed to be vehicle- and charger-agnostic, the challenges Rocsys faced bringing it to market, and what operators should consider when designing an efficient robotaxi depot. We also cover where the M1 is already deployed, what the AV depot of the future could look like, and the common mistakes companies make when planning fleet operations.
Finally, we zoom out to discuss the global robotaxi landscape, including the differences between the U.S. and Europe, what Europe needs to do to become more competitive, and Crijn’s vision for how automation will transform depot operations over the coming years.
