A MODEST PROPOSAL
The Two Conflicting Models of Jewish Safety in 2026

One thing I have been thinking about and am not QUITE ready to put on twitter … let’s talk Israel and the Diaspora Jews & Tucker Carlson… oh boy:

There is a real schism opening up between much of the Jewish diaspora left and much of the Israeli Jewish right. And it is not really about Bibi, or Gaza, or campus protests, or any of the day’s surface arguments. Those are accelerants. The real fight is deeper. It is a high-stakes and very personal feeling fight between two opposite Jewish theories of safety… here is my attempt at what is really going on:

* The Post WWII Liberal Consensus - Safety in Post-Nationalism – For roughly 80 years, the dominant moral narrative in the West has been: nationalism is dangerous, ethnic nationalism is even more dangerous, and the path to peace is to weaken thick national identities in favor of liberalism, pluralism, minority protections, and universal rights. This story deeply appealed to diaspora Jews for obvious reasons …the answer to antisemitism was not more nationalism. It was less. Less blood-and-soil identity. Less ethnic majoritarianism. More open societies. More neutral institutions. More universalism. More rules. More meritocracy. More tolerance.

This looked pretty good for a while but: (a) it is dependent on others / it is a civilization level tectonic move / beyond just the jewish community, so it requires a lot of trust in others and an ‘arrow of history’ rather than a cycle (b) 10/7 was a real blow to people’s sense of how it was going… the safety of the ‘coalition’ of minorities that Jews thought they had in the west… that is why it was such a wakeup call.

* Enter Tucker / Nationalism X.0 The “Tucker Carlsons of the world” are not just randomly becoming more nationalist. They are emerging out of a real vacuum, and a weakened memory from nearly a century ago. A lot of people — especially men, especially in the West, especially people with declining status and no larger story — are desperate for a more bounded source of identity, meaning, and belonging. Nationalism is one of the most powerful answers on offer. Sometimes cultural nationalism. Sometimes civil nationalism. Sometimes something uglier. I am not excusing this BUT I do think it is important to understand the meaning / sense of purpose and place / vacuum in which it is happening as a real lived experience for many… and why our moment is so different then the 1970s / 1980s…
* The Israeli Safety Framework - Safety In Nationhood And then there is the great Jewish irony at the center of all this: Israel is also a solution to antisemitism… just the opposite one. The diaspora answer to antisemitism is: weaken nationalism, protect minorities, universalize the rules, and Jews will be safer. The Israeli answer is almost the reverse: Jews are safest when Jews have power, borders, sovereignty, military capacity, and a state organized around Jewish peoplehood. That is the conflict. … not policy, not pro-this government, or that… which ironically is directionally in-line with this new-school nationalism of the Tucker crowd.

The deeper conflict is that these are fundamentally competing models of Jewish survival…. Each side thinks the other is not just wrong, but some combination of naive AND reckless - and the stakes feel practical not just intellectual right now

That is why this conflict feels so intense, and why it is getting worse so quickly. It is not just political disagreement. It is high-stakes model conflict. It is two different strategies for answering the same ancient question: how do Jews remain safe in a world that periodically turns on them?

One answer is: dissolve the danger by dissolving nationalism. The other is: survive the danger by becoming a nation. — Those models can coexist in periods of stability. But under stress, they start to attack each other (as we see now…) Because each one implies that the other is not just mistaken, but potentially suicidal / endangering the other…
