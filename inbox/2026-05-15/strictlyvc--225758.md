## General Catalyst Posted VC Rage Bait and It Worked, Especially on a16z

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/cf2844dc-3d3f-49de-9499-93a59d271655/Rage-bait__1_.jpeg?t=1778899912)
Caption: 

**By Julie Bort**

One of the most entertaining moments in VC this week was a piece of rage bait marketing from General Catalyst.

In a now-viral post on X that parodies the old Mac vs. PC commercials, the venture firm — better known as GC — posted a “VC vs GC” video on Wednesday. The VC was played by a tall actor in a baggy shirt and vest with a distinctly large, bald head — an apparent dig at Andreessen Horowitz co-founder Marc Andreessen. (But the real Andreessen never looks that disheveled).

The GC character was played by a man with a thick head of dark hair, white kicks, and a tendency to stare deeply into the camera. He was clearly supposed to represent actor Justin Long’s cooler, “hipper” Mac character from the original commercials, in contrast to John Hodgman’s straight-laced “square” PC persona.

## Massive Fundings

**E****lliptic**, a 13-year-old London company that provides blockchain analytics and compliance infrastructure for digital asset transactions, risk monitoring, and financial crime investigations, raised a $120 million round at a $670 million post-money valuation. The deal was led by **One Peak Partners**, with** Nasdaq Ventures, Deutsche Bank**, and the** British Business Bank** also participating. _[More here](https://www.elliptic.co/media-center/elliptic-secures-120-million-investment)_.

**Fasset**, a seven-year-old Los Angeles and Dubai startup that provides stablecoin-based banking, cross-border payments, and lending services for small and medium-sized businesses, raised a $51 million Series B round. Investors included **SBI Group, Investcorp**, and** Arz Portföy**. Crypto.news has _[more here](https://crypto.news/fasset-raises-51m-stablecoin-neobank-funding/)_.

**Gridcare**, a two-year-old startup based in Redwood City, CA, that uses physics-based AI to identify unused electrical grid capacity and accelerate power connections for data centers and AI infrastructure, raised a $64 million Series A round led by **Sutter Hill Ventures**, with **John Doerr, National Grid Partners, Future Energy Ventures, Emerson Collective**, and **Stanford University **as well as previous investors **Xora, Aina Ventures, Overture, Acclimate Ventures**, and** Clearvision Ventures** also piling on. Data Center Dynamics has _[more here](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/gridcare-raises-64m-in-series-a-funding-round/)_.

**Multiverse**, a 10-year-old London company that helps organizations identify workforce skill gaps and train employees in data, digital, and AI capabilities, raised a $70 million round at a $2.1 billion post-money valuation. Schroders Capital was the deal lead, with **General Catalyst, Lightspeed, D1 Capital Partners, Index Ventures, Bond**, and** StepStone Group **also participating. The company has raised a total of $570 million. Tech Funding News has _[more here](https://techfundingnews.com/euan-blairs-multiverse-raises-70m-at-2-1b-valuation-to-become-europes-ai-adoption-platform/)_.

**Rapido**, an 11-year-old Bengaluru startup that provides ride-hailing services for motorbikes, auto-rickshaws, and cars, raised a $240 million round at a $3 billion valuation led by **Prosus**, with **WestBridge Capital **and** Accel **also engaging. The financing was part of a larger $730 million primary and secondary transaction. TechCrunch has [more here](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/15/indian-uber-rival-rapido-raises-240m-at-3b-valuation/).

## Big-But-Not-Crazy-Big Fundings

**A****rkeus**, a six-year-old Melbourne startup that is developing AI-powered sensing systems that help drones and autonomous military platforms detect, classify, and track objects in low-visibility environments, raised an $18 million Series A round led by **QIC Ventures**, with** R+VC, Folklore Ventures, DYNE Ventures, Main Sequence Ventures, Salus Ventures**, and **Beaten Zone** also participating. _[More here](https://www.citybiz.co/article/846958/arkeus-raises-18m-series-a-to-scale-ai-powered-defense-sensing-systems/)_.

**Butterfly Medical**, a 12-year-old Boston and Israeli company that develops minimally invasive implantable devices that treat benign prostatic hyperplasia by retracting prostate tissue to restore urine flow, raised a $21 million Series C round. Investors included **European Innovation Council, Junson Capital, MedVenture**, and **New Rhein Healthcare Investors**. Medical Device Network has _[more here](https://www.medicaldevice-network.com/news/butterfly-medical-21m-funding-bph-device/)_.

**Crew Carbon**, a four-year-old Brooklyn startup that uses alkaline minerals to improve wastewater treatment efficiency and remove carbon dioxide during the treatment process, raised a $25 million round led by **Burnt Island Ventures**, with** AP Ventures, Sony Innovation Fund, Builders Vision, Kibo Invest, Idemitsu Ventures**, and** New York Ventures** as well as previous investors **Counteract, ANIMO Ventures, Connecticut Innovations, Ponderosa Ventures, and Echo River Capital **also taking part. _[More here](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/crew-raises-25m-of-funding-to-scale-wastewater-treatment-optimization-technology-as-climate-solution-302772585.html)_.

**Nord Quantique**, a six-year-old Québec startup that develops superconducting quantum computers, raised a $30 million round from **Fidelity** at a $1.4 billion post-money valuation. BetaKit has [more here](https://betakit.com/quantum-startup-nord-quantique-secures-1-4-billion-usd-valuation/).

**Novella**, a two-year-old New York startup that uses AI agents to automate underwriting analysis, policy processing, renewals, inspections, and other wholesale insurance brokerage workflows for complex commercial risks, raised a $21 million round led by **Brewer Lane Ventures**, with **Box Group, Crystal Venture Partners, SV Angel, Avid Ventures, Verissimo Ventures**, and **Blank Ventures** also contributing. CTech has _[more here](https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/bjo3aefyge)_.

**Osero**, a one-year-old Warsaw startup that is building infrastructure that lets wallets, exchanges, neobanks, and asset managers offer yield-bearing stablecoin products, raised a $13.5 million round co-led by **Sky Ecosystem **and** Plasma**. CoinDesk has _[more here](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/12/stablecoin-yield-infrastructure-project-raises-usd13-5m-in-round-led-by-sky-ecosystem)_.

**Tokaido Health**, a one-year-old San Francisco startup that uses AI and behavioral incentives to help employers steer patients toward lower-cost prescription drugs and care options, raised a $25 million round. Investors included **Primary Venture Partners, Norwest, Next Ventures, Constellation**, and **Scrub Capital**. _[More here](https://www.primary.vc/articles/tokaido-health-raises-25m)_.

**Turnkey**, a four-year-old New York startup that provides wallet and key-management infrastructure for crypto applications, including non-custodial wallets and automated onchain transactions, raised a $12.5 million round. Investors included **Archetype and Circle Ventures **as well as previous investors** Bain Capital Crypto, Lightspeed Faction, Galaxy Ventures, Sequoia Capital**, and** Variant**. The company has raised a total of $65+ million. CoinDesk has _[more here](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/06/turnkey-raises-usd12-5-million-in-round-backed-by-circle-ventures-and-sequoia-capital)_.

**Urologic Health**, a three-year-old startup based in Jersey City, NJ, that develops non-invasive bladder monitoring systems that collect bladder function data without catheter-based testing, raised an $11 million seed round led by an undisclosed strategic investor and including **Edge Medical Ventures, SHD Partners**, and** Longevity Venture Partners**. _[More here](https://www.citybiz.co/article/846655/urologic-health-raises-11-million-to-advance-non-invasive-bladder-monitoring-technology/)_.

**Xpanner**, a six-year-old startup based in Santa Fe Springs, CA, that develops AI-powered construction automation software and retrofit systems for heavy machinery, raised an $18 million Series B bridge round led by previous investor **Korea Investment Partners**, with** KB Investment** also participating. The company has raised a total of $38 million. Crunchbase News has _[more here](https://news.crunchbase.com/real-estate-property-tech/xpanner-automation-as-a-service-for-construction-sites-startup-funding-physical-ai-robotics/)_.

## Smaller Fundings

**Chromie Health**, a two-year-old New York startup that deploys SMS-based AI agents that help hospitals fill open nursing shifts and manage staffing workflows, raised a $2 million pre-seed round led by **AIX Ventures**. Fierce Healthcare has [more here](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/ai-and-machine-learning/chromie-health-earns-2m-pre-seed-funding-launches-sms-ai-powered-staffing).

**Flick**, a one-year-old San Francisco startup that enables filmmakers to direct and iterate on AI-generated films through cinematic editing and storytelling workflows, raised a $6 million seed round. Investors included **True Ventures, GV, Y Combinator, Lightspeed, Formosa Capital, Pioneer Fund, Olive Tree Capital**, and** N1**. _[More here](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260514408256/en/Flick-Raises-%246M-Seed-Round-to-Redefine-How-AI-Films-Are-Created)_.

**Ouinex**, a four-year-old Paris startup that operates a crypto trading platform for digital assets, forex, stocks, commodities, and derivatives using crypto collateral, raised a $3.5 million round from retail and professional traders using its own platform. The company has raised a total of $9 million. _[More here](https://www.finsmes.com/2026/05/ouinex-raises-3-5m-in-funding-round-total-reaches-9m.html)_.

**Twin Prime**, a one-year-old Palo Alto startup that is developing AI models that analyze and reason across military and security sensor data to support battlefield and operational decision-making, raised a $10 million pre-seed round. **Expeditions** was the deal lead, with **Theon International **also pitching in. Resilience Media has _[more here](https://resiliencemedia.co/expeditions-backs-frontier-defence-ai-lab-twin-prime-in-10m-pre-seed-raise/)_.
