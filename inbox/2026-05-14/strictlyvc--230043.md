## Cerebras Systems IPO Makes Billions for Benchmark, but VC Eric Vishria Almost Didn’t Take the Meeting

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/d4cca8a7-6bb8-4d03-ac88-fb40c85b7f61/Eric-Vishria-Andrew-Feldman-2__1_.jpeg?t=1778805314)
Caption: Image Credits: Eric Vishria

**By Julie Bort**

The Cerebras Systems IPO [was a smash hit on Thursday, generating billions](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/14/cerebras-raises-5-5b-kicking-off-2026s-ipo-season-with-a-bang/) for itself, its founders, and its major investors. 

Among the big winners is major shareholder Benchmark, which owns 9.5% of the company. One of the firm’s general partners, Eric Vishria, has been a Cerebras board member since 2016, the year the AI chipmaker was founded, having co-led its $25 million Series A round. 

But these billions only happened for Benchmark because Vishria met with the startup almost against his will, he told TechCrunch. 

“It was five founders and a deck, and it was our first hardware investment in 10 years,” Vishria told TechCrunch about that first meeting. “I had been a venture capitalist for like, 18 months.” (Prior to being a VC, Vishria sold the social browser startup he co-founded, RockMelt, [to Yahoo for a reported $60-$70 million in 2013](https://techcrunch.com/2013/08/02/yahoo-has-acquired-rockmelt-apps-to-shut-down-on-august-31st/).) 

Benchmark is famously selective in the companies it chooses, and backs hardware companies so rarely that Vishria was kicking himself for giving time to Cerebras. 

“Why did I take this meeting?” he kept muttering. At one point, he even messaged his assistant, who manages his calendar, and bugged her: “Why did you let me take this meeting?” Vishria recalls. 

But his grumpy attitude vanished by the third slide, as co-founder and CEO Andrew Feldman laid out Cerebras’ grand plans. 

Read more (https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/14/cerebras-ipo-makes-billions-for-benchmark-but-vc-eric-vishria-almost-didnt-take-the-meeting/)
