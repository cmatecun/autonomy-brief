Bezos’ Prometheus Raises $12B
It’s been just six months since Prometheus emerged from stealth with $6.2B in funding. Now, Jeff Bezos and Vik Bajaj’s industrial AI startup has raised another $12B [ https://substack.com/redirect/96e9f5ab-f392-433b-954a-e2c38e097cee?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] in its Series B at a $41B valuation. Back in November, there was very little disclosed about Prometheus, and not much has changed since. In Bezos’ defense, he did said the company isn’t being secretive, just heads down. The co-CEOs spoke with CNBC today [ https://substack.com/redirect/df93c0c2-4df6-4332-a0e0-07ecc6ca0304?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] to discuss the fundraise, with Bezos outlining what the company is actually working on.
Our goal at Prometheus is building a set of tools that accelerate that invention loop. How long does it take to improve something? How long does it take to go from idea to actually manufacturing something at rate and have a useful object… This is an age-old dream, the idea that you might build a set of tools that could actually do engineering, an artificial engineer. An artificial general engineer is a dream that we’ve had for decades, but it’s never really been possible. But now it is.
The news also had me revisiting a theory John and Jordi discussed on the show last year: could this be Jeff Bezos’ version of NeXT, the company Steve Jobs started after he was fired from Apple? Of course, Bezos wasn't fired from Amazon, but maybe Prometheus represents his return to day-to-day company building. — Jackson

Thrive X Launch
Thrive Capital just closed Thrive X [ https://substack.com/redirect/50589646-9c7c-4869-9ec3-809d7c832441?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ], a new fund exceeding $10B, its largest ever and nearly double the size of its last fund. It’s $1B for early-stage and $9B for growth. This comes as some of its biggest bets: OpenAI, Stripe, SpaceX, Databricks, Anduril, Cursor, etc. have become some of the defining private companies of the current cycle.
The strategy: concentrated, founder-loyal, and allergic to indexing the market. The “Fifth Avenue” strategy as discussed on Invest Like the Best.
“Concentration demands loyalty to the founders and missions we back.”
Josh Kushner also told Bloomberg [ https://substack.com/redirect/8843dafc-0f83-4539-b99a-286e5080135d?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] that the winners of the AI boom would “be bigger than we can ever imagine,” which pretty neatly explains the raise. — John
CAA Launches $250M Creator Fund
CAA is getting back together with TPG, at least for creators. The agency and TPG’s Integrated Media Company have formed Compound Creative Holdings [ https://substack.com/redirect/08132ae5-4306-420a-ad2e-2fa22fe3cd23?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ], a $250M holding company designed to invest in and acquire creator-led businesses. The focus is YouTubers, podcasters, and other digital-native entertainers who are starting to look like standalone media companies more than traditional solo influencers.
Compound will be led by CAA veteran Tucker Brown, who has already worked on financing for creator businesses including Dude Perfect and the MeidasTouch Network. The basic thesis is that there are now creators doing tens of millions in ad sales, but many still do not have the capital structure, M&A support, or operating infrastructure to scale into something more durable.
CAA co-Chairman Kevin Huvane said: “Creators around the world are building full-fledged media companies with direct audience connections and true ownership of their intellectual property.”
CAA already represents talent. TPG knows how to buy and scale assets. Compound is the bridge between the two. CAA and TPG are effectively saying: what if the next Oprah, Reese Witherspoon, or Barstool-style media empire starts on YouTube or a podcast feed, and we own part of the operating company? — John
Endurance Energy Raises $54M
Endurance Energy has raised a $54M Series A [ https://substack.com/redirect/379564eb-ad51-47f5-99b0-0b1ddc45ca0f?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] to go after offshore geothermal. The company was founded last year by Andrew Redd, a former SpaceX engineer who worked on Dragon and Starship, and the round was led by Founders Fund with participation from Ascend, Construct Capital, Felicis, First Round, Point72 Ventures, Riot Ventures, and Voyager Ventures.
Traditional geothermal is real, clean, and baseload, but geographically constrained. You usually need to drill deep into hot rock, often far from major demand centers. Endurance wants to move the whole thing offshore, where tectonic activity around the Ring of Fire brings heat much closer to the surface, and where coastal cities could theoretically tap into massive nearby resources.
25 employees now and notably 12 are former SpaceX employees. The SpaceX DNA playbook has worked many times: take an old industrial category, attack it from first principles, and assume the engineering is hard but not impossible.
CEO Andrew Redd estimates offshore geothermal potential around the Ring of Fire in the 6 terawatt range, which is enormous, even in the world of gigawatt data centers. For context, the world uses about 20 terawatts of energy on average across all sources. His bigger ambition is to “support any major coastal city on the Ring of Fire.” - probably makes desalination economically viable too. — John
