WWDC Foundations

*Ben Bajarin, welcome back to Stratechery.*

*BB:* Hello, Ben Thompson. Thanks for having me again.

*I’ve been wanting to have you back on for a while, and we finally got a good occasion this week, which is WWDC ( https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hF8swzNR1-o ) , so let’s start there. What was your overall take on the keynote, beyond it being shockingly short?*

*BB:* Shockingly short, which I thought was interesting, because any of us who’ve watched this for a while know WWDC used to be a product or vertical operating system set of narratives — here’s macOS, here’s iPadOS, here’s whatever. This was a platform conversation, and so I thought that was different.

My read on that — and I don’t think this was my overall take coming into this, but the realization afterwards — is that to do all the things we want them to succeed in or do with generative AI, and AI in a smart Siri, a lot needed to be re-architected at the platform level.

*And by platform, do you mean not just at the operating system level, but Apple as a platform broadly?*

*BB:* Correct. Every platform essentially needed to be re-architected, you probably felt this too — we have a lot of conversations, and they’re always like, “You know what, operating systems and platforms just get crusty, they’re old, they’ve been built on something that was great 10 years ago and not now”. This is the same problem that Microsoft’s been trying to go through with Windows to modernize it, a lot just needs to be modernized, and that’s hard to do when you’ve got billions of customers.

So that I thought was the take of, “We need to do these things” — when’s the last time Apple’s mentioned a CPU scheduler? They update CPU schedulers all the time — they just felt the need to call out that they’ve actually done it even more, and the index, when’s the last time they talked about, “Hey, we rebuilt the index”? That’s boring on the surface, but necessary to do all this stuff with Siri and Apple Intelligence.

*I thought the index was a really notable point, because if you understood broadly what the point of Siri was, it was notable that they spent so much time on the index. This was my overall take on the AI story, they’re perfecting a 2024 AI story, which is when they announced Apple Intelligence ( https://stratechery.com/2024/wwdc-apple-intelligence-apple-aggregates-ai/?access_token=eyJhbGciOiJSUzI1NiIsImtpZCI6InN0cmF0ZWNoZXJ5LnBhc3Nwb3J0Lm9ubGluZSIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJhdWQiOiJzdHJhdGVjaGVyeS5wYXNzcG9ydC5vbmxpbmUiLCJhenAiOiJIS0xjUzREd1Nod1AyWURLYmZQV00xIiwiZW50Ijp7InVyaSI6WyJodHRwczovL3N0cmF0ZWNoZXJ5LmNvbS8yMDI0L3d3ZGMtYXBwbGUtaW50ZWxsaWdlbmNlLWFwcGxlLWFnZ3JlZ2F0ZXMtYWkvIl19LCJleHAiOjE3ODM3NjQxODQsImlhdCI6MTc4MTE3MjE4NCwiaXNzIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9hcHAucGFzc3BvcnQub25saW5lL29hdXRoIiwic2NvcGUiOiJmZWVkOnJlYWQgYXJ0aWNsZTpyZWFkIGFzc2V0OnJlYWQgY2F0ZWdvcnk6cmVhZCBlbnRpdGxlbWVudHMiLCJzdWIiOiIwMTk1MTZmMS1hMzQ2LTc2MTQtOWU5Ni1iNjNiZDExZmU4NTYiLCJ1c2UiOiJhY2Nlc3MifQ.mn1gYV6f8OAgGob0NV3rqjROOBnyJSWMq597UznKUU69eM6DE-al6zK1pFSZ3WCYlJ__vE8B2t1xi5sfncNAprU0hvfrY_zj7N_UgaM4vQle50sGcNBI6uGaYgBPo59R-alExRuXDBbhTwwb09upzU-FFarP_PY8tWrp26cg7ZN9ms5IA4oteifsSS5frzWy4aHKooftLBgJxte9U1gVLGr1Wo5cuFp1se_M0CTlN61pH01xkaCjYvZhvzitr4k-Y_2_VlUFYNdj4tA4xltNZZOAZPW7NFF7cXrXpcKEkGVNtzp11tPbQN9UdmYVX8faQEm1HLe4YlArbvWBXBBZ0A ). This was basically, “Let’s do what we said in 2024, but for real this time”. To what extent was there the realization that the reason why Siri failed wasn’t just because the models were bad or whatever it might be, but because if we’re going to do RAG ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retrieval-augmented_generation ) , we actually have to have a functional search index, and they didn’t have that”?*

*BB:* I think it’s related — that’s exactly right. Obviously, we wanted them to get here sooner — I don’t think any of us perhaps appreciated the magnitude of the platform re-architecture that was needed to do this.

*What about us? What about Apple?*

*BB:* Well, that’s the thing. The broader question would be, “Could you have done some of this sooner? Is it all necessary?” — maybe that’s the case, but the reality is, and you’re exactly right, I would imagine that search, and semantic search, and better contextual search, is actually going to be one of the things that people do a lot of, and so having the index get redone was a key part of being able to do that. Maybe it wouldn’t have worked before.

*Well, all the Siri demos that are super obviously useful, and that, from what I’ve seen, seem to clearly work, are basically just fancy search at the end of the day. Is that a fair characterization?*

*BB:* Correct, yes, absolutely. And again, that’s one of the things where that’s been like a black hole for a lot of people — you lose a lot of stuff, you’re not organized.

*And also, if you ever tried to search in Messages, you realize that — I mean, that was almost like a tell, right?*

*BB:* It doesn’t work at all.

*BB:* Ever!

*So on what aspect is Siri going to be able to find it?*

*BB:* Yeah, 100%. Or I could say, “Search for something from Ben Thompson”, and you don’t show up at all. And I was like, “He’s in my contacts, how can you not find this message?”, it’s just stuff that now, in a gen AI world, you’d walk back and say, “If it didn’t do those things, I would view it as a failure”, and so it needs to do those things, and that’s why the platform needs to be updated.

So broad picture, taking a platform approach was kind of what they did, because all those pillars led up to now trying to mainstream generative AI and bring it to the masses — they had to do those first two things first.

*Do you think that is enough? Is actually just being, “State of the art in 2024 but it actually works, and you happen to own the operating system and have all that data” — do customers actually want anything more than that ( https://stratechery.com/2026/the-iphones-last-stand/?access_token=eyJhbGciOiJSUzI1NiIsImtpZCI6InN0cmF0ZWNoZXJ5LnBhc3Nwb3J0Lm9ubGluZSIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJhdWQiOiJzdHJhdGVjaGVyeS5wYXNzcG9ydC5vbmxpbmUiLCJhenAiOiJIS0xjUzREd1Nod1AyWURLYmZQV00xIiwiZW50Ijp7InVyaSI6WyJodHRwczovL3N0cmF0ZWNoZXJ5LmNvbS8yMDI2L3RoZS1pcGhvbmVzLWxhc3Qtc3RhbmQvIl19LCJleHAiOjE3ODM3NjQxODQsImlhdCI6MTc4MTE3MjE4NCwiaXNzIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly9hcHAucGFzc3BvcnQub25saW5lL29hdXRoIiwic2NvcGUiOiJmZWVkOnJlYWQgYXJ0aWNsZTpyZWFkIGFzc2V0OnJlYWQgY2F0ZWdvcnk6cmVhZCBlbnRpdGxlbWVudHMiLCJzdWIiOiIwMTk1MTZmMS1hMzQ2LTc2MTQtOWU5Ni1iNjNiZDExZmU4NTYiLCJ1c2UiOiJhY2Nlc3MifQ.XQX64FppDub-ukpDDPF2w8D9X-CQgn0XAKRk5rQJT_Zu3Ph-UQNu8NMUzY_dh-HfETFMW4syYzFDR88QFqD7R4D5yFNDUTHNNG8kLtQHcgDyTT5Dggu1i9JKBujoVILcvZJC0yWeBcKkUySQ1oePsDFmKumFbYcQ3TQnixvXBjpYnCvnbZVDTJMn0ZF02jg7wO9LpXWpmow0CK7uZn0BXqleFe9RVfxY2FIBptZub0Qi_AXCIbky_tw4TpcTgwaiewHJTVq1GTNz3ibOhyKxiPIXqg8JKL0HrICop5G69qxY51gYrM9nvIGdIsM6YNYSJwfT9jCSYJd0oPK6moONIQ ) ?*

*BB:* I think there’s a base level of expectation that my device does these things, and I think that’s where they landed. If you just look at even Gemini — Gemini is fine, it’s definitely not OpenAI or Claude in terms of its capabilities yet, it does what you need it to do when you’re searching and might want to interact with your calendar. These are basic things, and I also step back and say most consumers on the long tail of adoption are not using this for anything more than search. When they use GPT or Claude, it’s just search.

*The two big use cases that I seem to see for normal people are recipes and do-it-yourself projects, which are fancy search at the end of the day.*

*BB:* Yes, fancy search. So that’s why I think the interesting part here is, if we know that a lot of people kind of don’t do these things other than search — most people are not doing full workflow automation for their lives — they might do this for some work stuff, but they’re not doing it for their lives. That connecting to a consumer job, or the agent that I hire to go make a change in my calendar or find this image or have deeper semantic search — these are still new behaviors, and having a way that Apple mainstreams that in a way everybody can use it, for me, was always the key. Could they do it in a way that even grandma can use and find useful? That I just don’t think was an easy mountain to climb, and it looks like what they’ve got has gotten to a point where, honestly, it doesn’t always even mean you need to use Siri — it will be invisible in the background, but still be useful.

So this useful AI in an application, or applied AI, which is kind of the term I was using — if you just use robotics and applied robotics as a term, it moves from the lab to the mainstream — this is applied AI at a consumer level, and I think they got to that point and honestly, that’s all people will need. If they want to go off and do advanced other workflow stuff, there’s other models, and that’s totally fine.

*When do you think Apple realized they needed to have their own — because they tried to draw this clear line between world knowledge and personal knowledge in 2024 — when did they realize that actually this is a line that does not make sense at all?*

*BB:* I think for them — and we’ve talked about this too — there needs to be value in general search. The assumption when I heard, “Hey, we might be working with Gemini”, or when the rumor was out, was, “Oh cool, search is going to get good, because I use this on my device a lot”. So the idea is that they are bringing what they’re calling Apple world knowledge to this, and to some degree it sounds like it’s their index — they’re trying to separate themselves from Google. But that’s a core function that’s necessary, and to be honest with you, that’s a core function that’s necessary if we assume that they want to do glasses someday, and you might ask it about not just your food, but a flower or a bird or whatnot. So it needs to have that broad world knowledge, as they call it, and be something that they own and maintain.

Consumer vs. Enterprise AI

*Do you think consumers will ever care about agents and things like that?*

*BB:* No, I don’t. This is actually something I want to talk to you about.

*Oh, I’m getting interviewed now.*

*BB:* Yes! You and I were both born in the era of disruption theory ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disruptive_innovation ) and Jobs to Be Done ( https://www.christenseninstitute.org/theory/jobs-to-be-done/ ). Is this not one of the most cleanly interesting examples? I’m hiring something, I don’t need to think it’s an agent, but I’m hiring something, in this case it’s Siri, or is it GPT, or is it Anthropic, to go do something for me to get jobs done, and how I disintermediate what those jobs are. Is it summarize an email? Is it do this for my calendar? This to me is a super interesting jobs to be done, in its purest definition, of, “Why would I choose this control plane layer?”. Don’t even think agent — just think something I interact with, and it goes and does this stuff for me.
