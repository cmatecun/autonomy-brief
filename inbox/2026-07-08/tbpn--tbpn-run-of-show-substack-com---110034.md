Blue Origin is Raising Outside Capital!
Andrew Ross Sorkin has a scoop [ https://substack.com/redirect/824878e1-403c-479c-9b74-261fffeb2f78?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] in the NYT today about Blue Origin. The company is raising $10B in its first outside capital raise. 
Funny to think about this 25-year-old company as bootstrapped, but it really has been a solo Bezos megaproject for a quarter century now. He’s doubling down though and putting ~$2B into this round. Coatue is getting twice as much allocation with a nice $4B slice. 
Another interesting Coatue tidbit from the NYT piece: “Bezos’ family office is a major investor in Coatue’s Innovative Strategies Fund, which is focused on emerging-technology start-ups.” 
The valuation is expected to land at $130B. So a very soft launch into the private markets. Blue Origin is estimated to burn $5B this year and has burned ~$27B to date. Given how small the revenue base at Blue Origin is, some Elon fans are saying this makes SpaceX look cheap. Still, solid progress at Blue Origin even though they had that unfortunate landing pad setback. — John
Apple Winds Down Development of Cheaper Display for Apple Vision Pro
Apple has reportedly stopped development of a cheaper display for a lower-cost Vision Pro, according to MacRumors [ https://substack.com/redirect/583f91ec-0b9e-4436-b5c8-faa1127fd500?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ], with supplier Samsung Display expected to formally end the project by September.
The cheaper display would have cut the headset’s pixel density roughly in half, from 3,386 pixels per inch to around 1,600–1,700 PPI.
The move likely reflects Apple’s broader shift toward smart glasses: Bloomberg’s Mark Gurman previously reported that Apple paused work on a lighter, cheaper “Vision Air” in October 2025 to fast-track its Ray-Ban-style glasses effort.
This does not mean the Vision Pro itself has been canceled. Apple refreshed the headset in October 2025 with an M5 chip, and this report only suggests the cheaper display project is no longer moving forward. — Brandon
