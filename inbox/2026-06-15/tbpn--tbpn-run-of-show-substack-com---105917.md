The Hand Anthropic Was Dealt
Dario Amodei was notably not ringside at UFC Freedom 250. Mark Zuckerberg was. Will this matter long-term? Hard to say, but the aesthetic differences between Anthropic and the Trump administration are quite staggering. Bridging the gap might be difficult, but it’s clearly something all Americans should care about. Private enterprises should be able to flourish in the United States and the government should be able to enact reasonable regulations to protect America’s interests.
Catching up on the play-by-play.
March 4th: Anthropic receives DoW supply-chain-risk letter
April 7th: Mythos Preview announcement, alongside Project Glasswing
June 9th: Fable 5 Launch (Mythos with guardrails around cyber, bio, and AI research)
June 12th: Fable 5 suspended after Commerce Department issues export control directive.
Anthropic explained in a blog post [ https://substack.com/redirect/f7e240c6-7688-4e7f-8c9f-893dd6867543?j=eyJ1IjoiMXlnZjcifQ.KOUIeBfeCRzhIwFnKXyQigR4j0VpF-SZzascYhSZunw ] that the US government is restricting both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 from being used by any foreign national, whether inside or outside the US, including foreign national Anthropic employees. The sudden compliance burden here led to complete suspension of these models for all users, regardless of their nationality, and a firestorm of coverage over the weekend. The Information reported that Andy Jassy raised concerns with senior administration officials about the risk of jailbreaking Fable, Anthropic countered in their blog post saying: “We reviewed a demonstration of this specific technique being used to identify a small number of previously known, minor vulnerabilities. These vulnerabilities all appear relatively simple, and we have found that other publicly available models are able to discover them as well without requiring a bypass.”
The rollout of Fable 5 has been rocky, with incredible demos and benchmarks offset by the odd decision to silently degrade the quality of answers related to frontier AI development instead of just refusing the request, like cyber and bio prompts. The rationale behind AI development refusal is sound. If a model doesn’t let you hack a system, just have the model build you an unrestricted model that does let you hack your target. But the choice to silently degrade responses was not well received.
These are important issues and there’s another timeline where AI leaders are cut from the same cloth as America’s elected officials, but we are in a world where Anthropic has to make their case for safety in an age of RSI during a UFC fight on the White House lawn. Most of the big tech CEOs have figured out how to work with the administration effectively, showing up to large dinners, photo ops, and movie screenings to make their case about hot topics. Many of these CEOs also have business relationships with Anthropic, so you’d hope they can potentially bridge the gap a bit and land the conversation on more stable ground. At the same time, they are always renegotiating contracts, and competing across multiple markets, so expect the 4D chess to be on full display in Washington all summer long. — John
