## Key Developments

### Market Moves

- **Pony.ai + ComfortDelGro begin invite-only autonomous rides in Singapore** — a 12km route in Punggol's Northshore/Waterway area, saving commuters ~15 minutes vs. current public transit. Two Chinese AV companies (Pony.ai and WeRide) are now competing for the same district with different partners and pricing models (WeRide/Grab are free until mid-2026; Pony.ai/ComfortDelGro is moving toward paid service). *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **Atoms acquired Pronto to accelerate mining autonomy** — Pronto (previously an AV software company) becomes the core of Atoms Mining, one of three divisions dedicated to autonomous operations in mining environments. Mining offers near-term AV deployment advantages: fixed routes, no pedestrians, high labor costs, dangerous conditions. *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **Kodiak Robotics expanded driverless trucking to Ohio and Indiana**, extending commercial operations beyond the Sun Belt and into major Midwest freight corridors. Signals growing confidence in operating autonomous trucks in variable weather and more complex road networks. *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **Mariana Minerals partnered with Pronto to automate copper mine operations** — early commercial AV deployment in a controlled industrial environment. *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **MOIA America's LA deployment scale confirmed**: 100+ vehicles in testing, 500+ by Q3 2027, 1,000+ across additional US cities after that. Driverless operations targeted for 2027. *(AV Market Strategist, Driverless Digest)*

### Regulatory & Policy

- **Zagreb EU grant deadline forced Verne's rapid pivot to Chinese AV tech** — Verne (originally "Project 3 Mobility") received €179.5M in EU Recovery Fund grants to build a homegrown European robotaxi. After its purpose-built vehicle failed on stage in 2024, the company signed with Pony.ai and Uber on March 26 — 5 days before its grant milestone deadline of March 31. The CEO posted a paid-ride receipt dated March 30 on LinkedIn to prove the milestone was met. The deadline has since been extended to August 31. The result: EU taxpayer money funding Chinese vehicles (Arcfox Alpha T5), Chinese AV software (Pony.ai), on an American platform (Uber). *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **NHTSA closed its investigation into Tesla's remote driving feature** after the company implemented software updates. Removes one regulatory overhang for Tesla's robotaxi ambitions, though questions around unsupervised FSD and Austin launch timing remain open. *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **Fusion Processing advancing autonomous bus study at UK airports** (government-backed), targeting airside staff transport. Airports offer controlled environments and predictable routes suitable for early deployment. *(AV Market Strategist)*

### Technology & Product

- **Pony.ai launched PonyWorld 2.0** — an upgrade to its proprietary world model for L4 fleet training. The key addition is a "self-diagnosis" capability: the system reviews its own decisions, compares intent with outcomes, and identifies gaps to generate targeted data-collection tasks for human teams. Applied across its full driverless fleet since launch. *(AV Market Strategist)*
- **Waymo Nashville fleet structure detailed** — single fleet shared across both the Waymo One app and (upcoming) Lyft app. Lyft's FlexDrive subsidiary handles charging, cleaning, maintenance, and depot operations. This is the first real test of FlexDrive as a competitive asset in fleet management, distinct from just demand aggregation. *(AV Market Strategist)*

### Capital & Financials

## Strategic Takeaways

**The EU's AV industrial policy is delivering Chinese vehicles.** The Zagreb story exposes a fundamental tension in European AV strategy: grant money allocated to build local capability is accelerating Chinese AV companies instead. Pony.ai can now claim "first fare-charging robotaxi in Europe" — a headline that carries enormous weight in regulatory conversations, investor pitches, and future city negotiations across the continent. Verne survives with a path forward, but the biggest winner of the EU grant may be a Beijing-headquartered AV company. As European regulatory frameworks develop, policymakers face a real question: does "autonomy in Europe" require European technology, or just European operations? *(AV Market Strategist)*

**The Punggol district is becoming the world's first head-to-head Chinese AV war theater.** WeRide (with Grab, free rides, shuttle routes) and Pony.ai (with ComfortDelGro, invite-only, moving toward paid) are operating in the same small Singapore neighborhood with different commercial strategies. This mirrors their global positioning: WeRide is pursuing aggressive international diversification and operational scale; Pony.ai is building premium partnerships with established transit operators. How these strategies compare on ridership, safety record, and unit economics in the same geography will produce useful data for investors and regulators alike. *(AV Market Strategist)*

**The AV infrastructure layer is becoming a standalone investment thesis.** Terawatt's charging hub network (mentioned in the AV Market Strategist, powering major US AV fleets), Atoms Mining's acquisition of Pronto, and Waymo's pothole-data-sharing partnership with Waze all point to the same trend: the physical and data infrastructure that enables AV fleet operations is emerging as an investable category distinct from autonomy software itself. "Picks and shovels" AV plays — charging, data infrastructure, fleet operations — may offer more durable returns than betting on which AV stack wins. *(AV Market Strategist, Driverless Digest)*
